My career so far...

I first fell in love with design during my two years as the Editor-in-Chief of my high school newspaper. As I transitioned the newspaper's focus from print to digital, I became fascinated with the possibilities that website design offered. As I began my studies as a Frederick Honors Scholar at the University of Pittsburgh, I decided to major in Digital Narrative and Interactive Design with concentrations in UX/UI and web development.

In 2022, I became the Designer of Forbes & Fifth, an interdisciplinary research journal at the University of Pittsburgh. Beyond designing the layout, I also created dozens of graphics for each each, designed a new logo for the office, and updated the website.

Wanting to pivot from editorial design to UX/UI design, I began researching at the University of Pittsburgh School of Computing and Information that school year. With researchers in Pittsburgh and Ecuador, I spent the summer of 2023 designing an open-source interface for preserving Indigenous folklore and traditional ecological knowledge. Along with research, I also interned as a UX/UI designer at InvolveMINT, a nonprofit economic development agency in Pittsburgh that aims to award credits to volunteers for environmental work.
